Partners and Advocates

Partnerships and trusted voices are powerful tools in any campaign. When partners and advocates share your messages, they help cut through noise, expand reach, and build credibility â€” particularly in tight-knit or hard-to-reach communities.

What are Partnerships?

In communications, partnerships refer to organisations or individuals who can help amplify or co-deliver your messages. This can include:

  • Community groups and local networks
  • Influential individuals with lived experience
  • Trusted professional associations or peak bodies

Using Advocates

Advocates are third-party messengers who already have trust with your audience. Their support can help validate government action or messaging, especially when scepticism is high.

Top Tips

  • Identify aligned organisations or individuals early
  • Co-create messaging where possible to reflect their voice
  • Give clear guidance on timing, visuals and messages
  • Make it easy for advocates to support your work (e.g. tiles, packs, quotes)
  • Recognise and thank advocates publicly

Quick Wins with Partnerships

  • Use third-party social media pages or newsletters for fast amplification.
  • Ask partners to include a quote or foreword in your campaign materials.
  • Include partners in your launch or media activities to increase impact.
