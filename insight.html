Understanding Insight

Insight is the foundation of effective communications. It helps communicators understand their audience and how to reach them ‚Äî through data, research, evaluation and listening.

Insight isn't just about numbers. It's about drawing meaning and understanding from a range of sources, so we can develop communications that are audience-focused, evidence-based, and tailored to need.

Good communications start with strong insight. That means understanding your audience, their attitudes, needs and behaviours ‚Äî not just assumptions.

In government comms, this insight could come from research, community feedback, behavioural data, or direct consultation with those most affected.

The goal is simple: know what matters to your audience, and use that understanding to shape meaningful and measurable communication.

Read the full GCS guidance on using insight in government campaigns: GCS Insight Guidance

Tasmanian Government Insight Sources

Useful starting points for local data, audience insight and context:

  • State Growth: Population and Industry Data
  • RDAT: Regional Development Australia (Tasmania)
  • Tas Treasury: Economic Data
  • Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS)

You can use these sources to support campaign planning, define your audience, or track shifting attitudes over time.

Where to Start

  • Use existing data ‚Äì service stats, helpdesk logs, social listening
  • Talk to teams who engage with the audience ‚Äì frontline staff, case managers
  • Capture lived experience ‚Äì short interviews, focus groups or quotes
  • Map what you already know ‚Äì and highlight gaps

Even basic insight can make a campaign more relevant and effective.
