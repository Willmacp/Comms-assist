Why Internal Comms Matters

Internal communication helps inform, engage and align public sector employees with the goals and values of government. It supports change management, boosts staff morale, and builds trust across agencies and teams.

Principles

  • Be clear and consistent: Messages should be straightforward, timely, and reflect leadership direction.
  • Make it two-way: Good internal comms involves listening, not just broadcasting.
  • Use the right channels: Emails, intranets, newsletters, town halls, posters, videos â€“ each has its role.
  • Be inclusive: Reach all staff â€“ regardless of role, location, or work setup.

Common Formats

  • Weekly all-staff updates or newsletters
  • Intranet announcements and polls
  • Team briefings and talking points for managers
  • All-hands or town hall meetings
  • Internal campaigns for wellbeing, inclusion or change

Tasmanian Considerations

Many internal communications activities are handled by HR or executive teams. Comms staff should work closely with those areas to embed best practice and ensure communications are consistent across whole-of-government platforms like the intranet, DPAC comms channels, and departmental updates.
