Strategic Communications Overview

Strategic communication is the deliberate use of communication to advance organisational objectives. In government, this means providing timely, accurate and consistent information that supports policy, earns trust, and delivers measurable public value.

It involves identifying the right objectives, shaping the narrative, understanding audiences, and aligning all communication efforts ‚Äî from campaigns to stakeholder engagement ‚Äî to common goals. Strategic communication also ensures the right governance, roles and skills are in place to deliver this work effectively across departments.

Strategic communications in the public sector are not about short-term awareness alone ‚Äî they help shape policy outcomes, anticipate risks, and support long-term behaviour and social change.
