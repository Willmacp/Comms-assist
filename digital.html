Digital Overview

Digital communication is a vital part of modern government. This section helps teams across the Tasmanian Government use digital platforms to communicate effectively, accessibly and in line with audience expectations. It draws on the UK GCS guidance and local strategy frameworks.

Key Principles

  • Audience-first: All digital content should be designed with the user in mind â€” simple, accessible and timely.
  • Multichannel: Consider the full range of digital tools, including websites, social media, email, search and video.
  • Integrated: Digital activity should align with broader campaign goals and traditional media efforts.
  • Test and learn: Use data, A/B testing, and feedback to improve digital performance over time.
  • Security and standards: Follow Tasmanian Government digital and accessibility guidelines to ensure consistency and trust.

Tasmanian Context

Teams should align their work with the Tasmanian Government's Digital Strategy, particularly when developing online tools or platforms. Web content must also meet local accessibility standards and guidelines for digital inclusion.

Best Practice Areas

  • Website Content: Clear writing, mobile-friendly design, and logical structure.
  • Social Media: Consistent tone and timely updates. Use engagement insights to refine strategy.
  • Email Campaigns: Track open rates, click-throughs and unsubscribe patterns. Test subject lines and calls-to-action.
  • Search Optimisation: Use plain English, metadata and headings to help users find your content.
  • Analytics and Reporting: Tools like Google Analytics or social dashboards help assess and improve impact.
