🧰 Starter Kit for Smaller Agencies

Simple tools to help you communicate clearly and confidently — even without a comms team.

👋 Who this is for

This kit is for small Tasmanian Government agencies, statutory bodies, or teams who:

  • Don’t have a dedicated communications officer or team
  • Need to communicate with the public, stakeholders, or staff
  • Want to improve their communication clarity, consistency, and professionalism

You don’t need to be a communications expert — this kit gives you the basics to do it well.

✅ What’s included

  • Quick-start campaign checklist
    A one-pager to guide you through planning a communication — whether it’s a notice, update, or launch.
  • Simple template pack
    Includes media releases, social post planners, stakeholder emails, staff updates, and a comms calendar.
  • Tone and Style Guide (plain English)
    Write clearly, respectfully, and with a human voice — even on tricky topics.
  • Where to get help
    Contact points, branding kits, and what to do when media call.
  • Approval & Governance 101
    Understand the basics of sign-off and risk management for your comms.

This kit gives you a head start — but the full Comms Assist tool has even more templates, guidance, and examples.

📌 Key tips for small teams

  • Start small and clear. You don’t need a big campaign — sometimes a timely update is enough.
  • Use what already exists. This site has templates and examples — just adapt them.
  • Think audience-first. Ask: “Who is this for, and what do they need to know?”
  • Drop the jargon. Use plain language, short sentences, and everyday words.
  • Ask for help. Check the Community section or your departmental comms contact.

✍️ Tone and Style: Writing Plainly and Professionally

Clear, consistent writing helps your message land better — even more so if you're working without a comms team.

We recommend:

  • Writing in plain English (short sentences, everyday words, no jargon)
  • Using an active voice (e.g. “We released the update” not “The update was released”)
  • Following a consistent format for punctuation, capitalisation, and numbers

🔗 For best results, follow the State Growth Writing Style Guide (PDF), which outlines how to write in a professional and accessible way for government audiences.
