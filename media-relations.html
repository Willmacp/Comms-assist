Overview

Strong media handling builds trust, supports transparency, and maximises the reach and impact of your work. This section covers proactive and reactive media relations guidance tailored for Tasmanian Government communicators.

For central Tasmanian Government communications coordination, visit the DPAC Communications Unit.

Included in this section

  • Press release templates and examples
  • Interview briefing tips and checklists
  • Guidance on managing reactive queries and building proactive media strategies
  • Tips for evaluating media coverage and setting KPIs
  • Link to GCS guidance on Media Relations

Proactive media management

Proactive media is about shaping the narrative before others do. Key elements include building journalist relationships, planning targeted media drops, and aligning stories with public interest.

Downloadable templates and checklists will be added shortly.

Reactive and issues handling

When managing reactive stories or enquiries, having a clear internal process is key. A standardised briefing template and holding lines document can speed up response time and maintain consistency across departments.

Resources will be linked here when available.
